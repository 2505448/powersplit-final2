Why shared houses like PowerSplit

The goal is simple: nobody overpays, nobody feels blamed, and the numbers are clear enough that the bill can be settled in seconds.

Per-device tracking

Plug smart adapters into high-usage devices like heaters, gaming PCs and tumble dryers. Each one is linked to a person or shared between everyone.

Neutral maths

PowerSplit converts kWh into an unbiased split. Shared devices default to equal shares, and the group can adjust the ratios if you all agree.

One tap to settle

At the end of the month, everyone receives the same clear breakdown and pays their share online. No more chasing screenshots or doing mental arithmetic.

Starter Kit + App

PowerSplit is built around one simple idea: combine a small amount of smart hardware with a clean, shared dashboard that everyone in the house can understand.

What’s in the Starter Kit

  • 4× smart plugs — for heaters, consoles, PCs or other high-draw devices.
  • 1× mains clamp — tracks overall usage so the numbers stay grounded in reality.
  • Label set — shows at a glance who owns what, and which plugs are shared.
Kits are tuned for 3–5 person homes. Larger houses can add extra plugs later.

What the app gives you

  • Assign devices to people or mark them as shared in a couple of taps.
  • See a live estimate of each person’s share for the current month.
  • Export a clean summary for your group chat or landlord whenever you need it.

      How PowerSplit works in practice

      The aim is to make the setup feel like setting up a console or a smart speaker — not like installing a “smart meter project”.

      Four simple steps

      1. Plug in the hardware

      Plug smart adapters into the biggest energy users and clip the mains clamp around your incoming feed.

      2. Add your household

      Create a household in the app, name it (“Flat 12 King Street”), and invite your housemates by email.

      3. Assign devices

      For each smart plug, choose who owns it or mark it as shared. PowerSplit handles the kWh and tariff maths.

      4. Settle up

      When the bill arrives, everyone gets a link with their share and can pay from their phone in under a minute.

      For different types of homes

      In student flats, PowerSplit keeps things friendly by making it obvious how much heaters, consoles and late-night study sessions actually cost — before anyone feels taken advantage of.

      Privacy & control

      • Each person can see how their own devices contribute to the bill.
      • Household owners choose whether to grant landlord access to high-level summaries.
      • Usage is shown in kWh and cost, not in terms of “behaviour” or “judgement”.
